Review Guidelines

Comprehensive guidelines for evaluating submitted abstracts

Your Role as a Reviewer

Purpose: Review submitted abstracts, provide constructive feedback, and help determine which research should be presented at the UWI Principal's Research Awards & Festival.

Responsibility: As a reviewer, you play a critical role in maintaining the quality and relevance of research presented at our festival. Your thorough, fair, and constructive evaluations help authors improve their work and inform final editorial decisions.

Review Workflow
  1. View Assigned Submissions: Access abstracts assigned to your review theme from your dashboard
  2. Review Abstract Details: Read the full abstract, author information, and supplementary materials
  3. Evaluate Against Criteria: Assess the submission using the evaluation rubric below
  4. Provide Feedback: Write comprehensive comments addressing strengths and areas for improvement
  5. Submit Decision: Choose one of three actions:
    • Approve: Research is suitable for presentation as-is
    • Request Changes: Research has merit but requires revisions before acceptance
    • Recommend Alternative Format: Suggest changing presentation format (e.g., from oral to poster)
  6. Monitor Progress: Track review status and view consolidated feedback once all reviewers complete their evaluations
Evaluation Criteria & Scoring Rubric

Please evaluate each abstract on the following criteria using the scale provided (1 = Poor, 5 = Excellent):

1. Research Quality & Innovation

Definition: The originality, significance, and rigor of the research methodology and findings.

Poor (1): Lacks originality; limited innovation; methodological concerns
Fair (2): Some originality; incremental contributions; minor methodological issues
Good (3): Clear innovation; solid methodology; meaningful contributions
Very Good (4): Notable innovation; strong methodology; significant contributions
Excellent (5): Highly original; rigorous methodology; substantial impact potential
2. Relevance to Theme

Definition: How well the research aligns with conference themes and audience interests.

Poor (1): Minimal relevance to conference theme
Fair (2): Tangential connection to theme; limited audience interest
Good (3): Clear alignment with theme; relevant to audience
Very Good (4): Strong alignment; highly relevant and timely
Excellent (5): Exceptional relevance; addresses current priorities and interests
3. Clarity of Presentation

Definition: How clearly the research objectives, methods, and findings are communicated in the abstract.

Poor (1): Difficult to understand; unclear objectives and conclusions
Fair (2): Generally understandable; some clarity issues
Good (3): Clear presentation; minor wording improvements needed
Very Good (4): Well-written; clearly structured; professional quality
Excellent (5): Exceptionally clear; well-organized; easy to follow
4. Feasibility & Completeness

Definition: Whether the research scope is realistic and the abstract provides sufficient detail about methods and expected outcomes.

Poor (1): Unrealistic scope; insufficient detail; incomplete findings
Fair (2): Scope concerns; missing important details
Good (3): Realistic scope; adequate detail provided
Very Good (4): Well-scoped; comprehensive detail; clear outcomes
Excellent (5): Exemplary scope; thorough detail; complete findings
Best Practices for Reviewers
When Reviewing, Please:
  • Be Constructive: Provide specific suggestions for improvement, not just criticism
  • Be Fair & Objective: Evaluate based on merit and criteria, not personal preferences
  • Be Timely: Complete your reviews within the specified timeframe
  • Consider Context: Evaluate research relative to the author's field and experience level
  • Explain Your Reasoning: Justify your scores with specific examples and feedback
  • Respect Confidentiality: Do not disclose reviewer identities or reviews to anyone outside the review process
  • Declare Conflicts: Notify the review coordinator if you have a conflict of interest with an author
  • Provide Actionable Feedback: Give authors specific guidance on how to address concerns
Understanding Review Decisions
Approve

Research meets quality standards and is suitable for presentation. Author may proceed with presentation preparation.

Request Changes

Research has merit but requires revisions. Author will resubmit with improvements addressing your feedback.

Alternative Format

Research is acceptable but better suited as a poster instead of oral presentation (or vice versa).

Timeline & Expectations
Review Period: 2-3 weeks from assignment date
Expected Turnaround: Complete each review within 3-5 business days of assignment
Time per Review: 30-45 minutes per abstract recommended
Number of Abstracts: Varies by theme and conference size; typically 5-15 abstracts per reviewer
Feedback Detail: Minimum 150-300 words of constructive feedback per review
Questions or Issues?

Need clarification on guidelines? Contact the Review Coordinator

Technical issues with the platform? Submit a support ticket

Conflict of interest concern? Notify the Review Coordinator immediately
